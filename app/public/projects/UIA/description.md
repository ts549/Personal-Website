UIA is a coding agent that edits your site directly through the UI. Instead of describing what you want in a chat box, you point at the thing you want to change and tell UIA what to do. It then reaches into the underlying code and implements the change across both frontend and backend.

It is built for developers who are onboarding onto a team with monolith repos that would take weeks to fully understand, or non-engineers who can describe a change but can't code.

UIA is one of the projects I really wanted to ship and didn't get to. Halfway through building it, Cursor 2.0 came out and effectively shipped the same direction I was betting on, and the idea collapsed overnight. In hindsight, it should have been easy to see that this was the direction some coding agents would be heading, but in the moment I got blinded by how much I liked the idea and really wanted to make it work. Regardless, it was a good lesson for me to do my market research before diving head first into an idea.
